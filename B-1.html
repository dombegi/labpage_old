Devices

NAGDIS-PG

NAGDIS-I has been combined with Plasma gun, namely NAGDIS-PG(Plasma Gun).

NAGDIS-I

This is a rectilinear type divertor and plasma simulation device. The name NAGDIS stands for The Nagoya Divertor Simulator. The purpose of this device is to do the fundamental experiment of high temperature plasma simulation. As for feature of this device, access to the device is good in comparison with that of the large-sized annular device and superior in adaptability. With the stationary temperature plasma type, it is possible to research the interaction between the plasma and solid wall in the divertor region.

Magnetized Coexial Plasma Gun

This device can create high temperature/high density(τ = 200 μrm / n e ∼ 10 22 m -3 ) plasma. Three types of power supply(Plasma Gun supply, Gas Puff, Magnetic Bias) with a appropriate timing can produce the magnetic field, which create plasmoid. The plasmoid is sent into the cylinder of NAGDIS-I.

Main experiment outline

  • Fundamental experiments on divertor
  • Damage on reactor wall substance caused by DC high heat flux plasma
  • Plasma-surface interaction

NAGDIS-II

The name NAGDIS stands for The Nagoya Divertor Simulator. This is a also a linear type divertor and plasma simulation device with the modification of NAGDIS-I especially, it is possible to make ion temperature high, making use of ion cyclotron heating. The plasma thermal current can be control by the gas flow. The use of NAGDIS-I is limited by the high frequency induction heating by the ion cyclotron wave. NAGDIS-II are equipped with the various instruments as scanning probe and probe array (the end plate), such as CCD and spectroscope. That is why, it can measure the temperature and plasma density (electron density, ion density etc.) and neutral particle time. In addition, diameter direction distribution and various spectrum measurements of electron temperature near the end plate is also possible.

Main experiment outline

  • Damage on reactor wall substance caused by high heat flux plasma
  • Basic Characteristics of Detached Plasma
  • Molecular Activated Recombination in Detached Plasmas
  • Dynamics of recombining plasmas, etc.
